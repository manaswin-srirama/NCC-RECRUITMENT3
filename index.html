Why NCC Was Formed & Our Legacy

The National Cadet Corps (NCC) was formed with the vision of developing character, comradeship, discipline, leadership, secular outlook, spirit of adventure, and ideals of selfless service among the youth of the country.

Post-independence, India needed a unified organization to channel the energy of youth towards nation-building. The NCC serves as a bridge between the military and civilian sectors, creating responsible citizens who can contribute to national development.

üìöJourney of NCC Since 1948

Established under the National Cadet Corps Act of 1948, the NCC replaced the University Training Corps and has since evolved into India's largest uniformed youth organization. From rapid expansion across schools and colleges in the 1950s-60s to gaining international recognition for its unique civilian-military cooperation model in the 1970s, the NCC has continuously adapted to contemporary challenges while maintaining its core values and traditions.

"The safety, honour and welfare of your country come first, always and every time."

About NCC

Shape Your Future

Develop Core Values

The NCC is a premier organization that instills values of Discipline, Leadership, and Comradeship. Cadets learn to work as a team, take initiative, and develop a strong sense of national pride and selfless service (Seva Paramo Dharma).

Unique Opportunities

Gain hands-on experience in adventure activities like trekking, parasailing, and rock climbing. Participate in prestigious national camps such as the Republic Day Camp (RDC) in Delhi, and even get a chance to represent India internationally through the Youth Exchange Program (YEP).

Career Advantages

NCC 'B' & 'C' certificate holders receive bonus marks/points in various government job applications, including direct entry opportunities in the Indian Armed Forces. It's a significant stepping stone for a distinguished career.

The NCC Uniform: More Than Just Attire

The NCC uniform is a powerful symbol of dedication, unity, and honor. When you wear it, you embody the values of discipline and commitment while joining a proud tradition of excellence shared by millions of cadets before you.

More than clothing, the uniform represents your responsibility to make a positive impact on society and uphold the cherished ethos of the NCC. It's a reminder that you are part of something greater‚Äîa movement dedicated to nation-building and service.
